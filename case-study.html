Lexiland:Empowering Kids with Dyslexia Through Design

"Learning ought to be enjoyable rather than discouraging."Approximately 10% of children worldwide suffer from dyslexia, which makes it challenging to focus, read, and spell. They are frequently overloaded with text-heavy tasks and cluttered interfaces in traditional learning apps.

Duration: 1 months
Role: UI Designer and researcher
Industry: SaaS / Productivity

The Challenge

Problem Statement

Children with dyslexia often face unique challenges that can make learning feel discouraging. They may struggle to recognize words and letters, leading to slower reading progress and frequent frustration. Over time, this can lower their motivation and confidence, as repeated difficulties create a sense of persistent failure. Complex designs, cluttered layouts, or long blocks of text can easily overwhelm them, making it harder to stay focused. In traditional classroom settings, these struggles can also lead to anxiety, as children may feel pressured to keep up with their peers highlighting the importance of creating a learning environment that feels supportive, simple, and engaging.

Research & Discovery

Icons

I used simple, high-contrast icons from the Material Design icon set, ensuring each icon is instantly recognizable and supports quick navigation. The icons are rounded and minimal, reducing cognitive load while maintaining a playful, child-friendly feel.

Competitive Analysis

A competitive analysis of apps like Nessy Learning and Ghotit showed a lack of engaging visuals and consistent accessibility features. LexiLand addresses this by blending gamified learning, calming visuals, and simple navigation.

Fonts

For LexiLand, we chose the Lexend font, specifically designed to improve readability for individuals with dyslexia. Its wider letter spacing and clear, rounded forms make it easier for children to distinguish letters and words without visual strain.

The Solution

Lexiland:Empowering Kids with Dyslexia Through Design

LexiLand was created as an inclusive digital space designed specifically for children with dyslexia. Our goal was to make reading and learning enjoyable through accessible design and engaging interactivity. We implemented the Lexend font to enhance readability, used soft pastel backgrounds to reduce visual stress, and maintained ample spacing for easier focus. Interactive features like mini games, story time, and calm visuals were added to make learning feel playful rather than overwhelming. By combining accessibility principles with fun, child-centered design, LexiLand transforms the reading experience into something empowering, confidence-building, and joyful for every learner.
